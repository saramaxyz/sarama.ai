An AI research lab for
interspecies communication. Our first instrument is a dog collar.

Sarama studies non-human communication at consumer scale. We spent years decoding whales and dolphins. We still could not understand our own dogs. The collar is the unlock. The lab is the point.

Thesis

Animal communication has been bottlenecked by data, not by AI. No bark means the same thing across all dogs in all contexts. Meaning is contextual, causal, and individual. The path forward is multimodal, longitudinal, naturalistic data from tens of thousands of animals living their normal lives.

Program

The instrument is the data engine.

Vocalization

Repertoire, not dictionary

YAMNet-based segmentation, MFCC embeddings, and unsupervised clustering to recover per-dog vocal repertoires, not a universal bark-to-English lookup.

Multimodal

Audio, video, motion, physiology

Time-aligned signals on a single device: directional microphone, on-device visual features, IMU, and physiological proxies. Meaning lives in the joint distribution.

Individual

Per-dog causal baselines

Acoustic fingerprinting, individual-ID models, and routine-aware context. Reliable interpretation of one dog is built from that dog's history, not a generic prior.

Edge

On-device feature extraction

Inference compressed to ARM Cortex-M class hardware. Devices transmit features, not raw audio or video, so household-scale data collection is ethically tractable.

Feedback Loop

Owners as soft labels

The earlier app taught us labels reflect owner expectation, not dog meaning. Ground truth now comes from sensor-grounded signals; owner feedback is treated as soft, biased prior.

Open Science

Negative results in public

The pivot from bark-labeling to sensor-grounded modeling is on the record. We publish methods, datasets, and what didn't work, because the science isn't settled.

Instrument

Sarama Uplift

A scientific instrument worn on the collar. The first plausible path to consumer-scale, multimodal, longitudinal data on non-human behavior, shipped inside a product owners want for their own reasons.

Five time-aligned signals

Directional audio, on-device visual features, IMU, physiological proxies, environmental context. One device, one clock.

Hybrid edge-server architecture

On-collar inference for features. Server-side causal modeling for per-individual longitudinal baselines.

Privacy by construction

Raw audio and video never leave the device. Features only. Household-scale data collection without household-scale surveillance.

Translations, gated by confidence

For the first one to two months the app surfaces only health, emotional state, and activity. Translations appear when the per-dog causal model crosses 95% confidence, with context, body language, environment, time of day, attached to every call.
