We listen to the signals

Sarama is a research lab at the intersection of bioacoustics, machine learning, and animal welfare. We decode vocalization, breath, and movement to surface emotional and physiological state in real time.

Mission

The next frontier of intelligence isn't a larger model, it's a wider listener. We build recognition where others build control. From reaction to understanding. From tracking to care as a form of intelligence.

Research

Three modalities. One signal.

Vocalization

Bioacoustic models for canine communication

On-device models that interpret bark, whine, growl, and breath as continuous emotional signal, not discrete categories.

Movement

Behavioral pattern recognition

Longitudinal analysis of gait, posture, and rest-activity cycles to detect subtle shifts in wellness before clinical symptoms appear.

Physiology

Breath and stress biomarkers

Non-invasive respiratory pattern analysis and heart-rate-variability tracking to surface pain, anxiety, and early illness indicators.

Edge ML

On-device inference

Models optimized for ARM Cortex-M and Apple Silicon. Inference runs locally, your animal's data never leaves the device.

Longitudinal

Personalized baselines

Every animal has a unique signature. Our models learn individual baselines over weeks and months, adapting to personality and age.

Open Science

Publications and datasets

We publish our methods, datasets, and negative results. Understanding other species requires collective effort, not trade secrets.
